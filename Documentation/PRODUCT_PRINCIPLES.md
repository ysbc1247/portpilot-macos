# Product principles

1. **Explain before acting.** Show the observed listener, process identity, controlling owner, confidence, and action rationale before offering control.
2. **Observation is not management.** A process found by `lsof` is evidence. Only an explicit, reviewed, exactly validated definition is a DevBerth managed service.
3. **Restart is a trust claim.** Restart is available only when DevBerth can prove what it will start, where, with which discrete arguments, required secrets, readiness rules, and shutdown scope.
4. **Control the owner layer.** Container, Compose, managed group, tunnel, forward, and host-process actions use their verified controlling boundary. DevBerth does not substitute a convenient PID.
5. **Refusal is a valid product outcome.** Missing or stale evidence disables an action with a reason; the app does not weaken verification to complete a request.
6. **Local and account-free.** Runtime and project evidence stays on the Mac. Keychain stores secrets. No cloud account or upload path is required.
7. **Projects are topology; sessions are intent.** Projects express durable service/dependency organization. Sessions capture a desired managed-service state and restore it through a fresh preview.
8. **Evidence remains bounded.** Runtime snapshots and resource samples are transient; lifecycle history, ownership evidence, incidents, logs, and caches have explicit limits and invalidation.
9. **Native density with readable certainty.** Use standard macOS tables, lists, inspectors, keyboard navigation, menus, and accessibility. Status text must communicate meaning without relying on color.
10. **Compatibility is deliberate.** Shipped schemas and legacy identity mappings remain immutable compatibility boundaries; product terminology may improve without silently resetting user data.
